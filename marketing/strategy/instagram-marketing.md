# Pillar — Instagram Marketing Context

## Account Overview

Pillar is an AI companion app for young Indians (22–40). The Instagram account is being built ahead of the Google Play Store launch. All content is pre-launch — the CTA is currently:

> **"Pillar App is coming to Google Playstore soon. Follow for updates!"**

Once the app launches, this will change to:
> **"Download Pillar App from Google Playstore now."**

---

## Brand Voice

- **Language:** Hinglish (mix of Hindi and English) — not fully Hindi, not fully English
- **Tone:** Warm, honest, non-judgmental. Feels like a friend talking, not a brand
- **No emojis** in post copy (keep it clean and minimal)
- **Short paragraphs** — every line is its own breath
- **Never salesy** — lead with the emotion/pain point, introduce the solution gently

## Content Formats Used

### 1. Pain Point Hook Posts (Photo + Text overlay)
Real/AI-generated photo of a person in a relatable situation. Bold white Hinglish text overlay on left side. Small italic subtext below. Pillar. logo bottom left.

**Best performers of this type:** Post 1 (pehla message), Post 2 (2am fight), Post 3 (Sunday dread)

### 2. Solution Posts (Same photo + Chat overlay)
Same photo/person as the pain point post. Chat conversation with the coach appears as overlay. Headline changes to the solution/CTA. Creates visual continuity — problem → solution in same world.

**Pairs:**
- Post 1 → Post 7 (Maya, dating, pehla message)
- Post 3 → Post 8 (Kabir, career, Sunday dread)
- Post 2 → Post 9 (Mira, relationship, fight)

### 3. Coach Introduction Posts (Coach photo + text)
AI-generated cinematic photo of coach. Left 1/3rd has text, right 2/3rd has photo. Person positioned right, facing slightly left toward text. Open negative space on left for copy.

### 4. Dark Background + 3D Asset Posts
Pure dark background (`#080806`). Bold headline top. 3D object centre with matching glow. Pillar. logo bottom right. No photo of person — concept-driven.

**Series so far:**
- Broken heart → "You can't live like this forever." (Maya)
- Cracked compass → "Stop running from your potential." (Kabir)
- Hourglass → "Are you scared you are going to fail?" (General)
- *Planned:* Cracked mirror → "Are you losing yourself in love?" (Mira)

### 5. Brand/Feature Posts
Dark background, text only or with icons. Explains what Pillar is and how it works.

## AI Image Generation

### Coach Photos
Generated using **Nanobanana** with a reference photo uploaded for face matching.

**Prompt structure:**
> Full body shot of the [man/woman] in the reference photo, [pose] in [environment]. Positioned in the right 1/3rd of the frame, body angled slightly toward the left, looking [expression] at camera. Left 2/3rds is open negative space. Wearing [clothing]. [Lighting]. Shot on 85mm lens, shallow depth of field, cinematic, hyperrealistic photography. Keep the face identical to the reference. Do not alter facial features.

**Environments chosen:**
- Mira → Warm coaching office, bookshelves, natural light from window

## Paid Ads Strategy

**Platform:** Meta (Facebook + Instagram)

**Exploration phase budget:** ₹600–800/day total across all ads
**Per ad spend:** ₹50–150/day during testing
**Duration:** 7–10 days to find winners

**Campaign structure:**
- 1 Campaign (App Installs)
- 5 Ad Sets (grouped by angle — Mira, Kabir, Maya, Anya, General)
- 2 ads per ad set (English + Hinglish versions)

**Key metrics to watch:**
- CTR: >1.5% is good
- CPC: ₹5–20 is normal in India
- CPI (Cost Per Install): Target below ₹40–60
- Hook rate: 3-second video views %

**Kill ads above ₹80 CPI after 7 days. Double down on winners at ₹300–500/day each.**

**Don't judge ads before Day 5** — Meta's learning phase takes 3–4 days.

**Business model note:** Target conversion = ₹99/month autopay after conversations.
CPPU (Cost Per Paying User) must be below ₹1,000 for economics to work long term.
Retention of 12+ months needed for profitability at ₹99/month price point.
