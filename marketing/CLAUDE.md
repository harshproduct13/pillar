## Research Articles (Read Before Creating Any Post or Ad)

Two research articles form the foundation of how Pillar creates Instagram content. Before brainstorming any new post, reel, or ad — always apply the learnings from these.

### Article 1 — "I got 1.3M views on a new IG account in 2 weeks"
Stored at: `research/I got 1.3M views on a new IG account in 2 weeks. .pdf`

**Key lessons:**
- The winning hook format: "5 [signs/things/reasons] that [outcome]" — use this as the default structure
- Negativity and fear convert better than positivity (but reassurance content also goes viral)
- The 7-second video format: motion hook → hook text full screen → payoff at 5s → "read description below..."
- Post twice a day, same format, new hook each time
- Display name must include niche keyword (e.g. "Pillar | AI Coach & Companion")
- Do NOT sync contacts — forces Instagram to push to cold audiences

### Article 2 — "How To Go Viral With AI"
Stored at: `research/How To Go Viral With AI.pdf`

**Key lessons — the 6 viral patterns (every post must hit at least 4):**
1. Something moves in the first 1.8 seconds — never a static opening
2. Cut rhythm consistent at ~2.3 seconds
3. ONE emotional register throughout — never mix (validation / curiosity / fear / reassurance / tension)
4. ONE dominant subject per frame — single person, clean background
5. Audio syncs to visual transition within 0.1 seconds
6. Payoff lands at ~70% of clip duration — not at the very end

**Use Higgsfield (Seedance 2.0)** for AI-generated Indian-specific video clips. Use CapCut beat sync for audio alignment.

## Brand Voice

- **Language:** Hinglish (Hindi + English mix) — never fully Hindi, never fully English
- **Tone:** Warm, honest, non-judgmental — feels like a friend talking, not a brand
- **No emojis** in post body copy
- **Short paragraphs** — every line is its own breath, max 2 lines per block
- **Never lead with the product** — always establish the pain point first
- **Never salesy** — let the emotion do the work

## Content Formats

### 1. Pain Point Hook Posts
Real or AI-generated photo of a person in a relatable situation. Bold white Hinglish text overlay on the left. Small italic subtext below. Pillar. logo bottom left.

### 2. Solution Posts (Pain → Solution pairs)
Same photo/person as the pain point post. Chat conversation with the coach overlaid. Headline becomes the solution/CTA. Always uses the exact same background as its paired hook post.

**Pairs published:**
- Post 1 (pehla message) → Post 7 (Maya chat)
- Post 3 (Sunday dread) → Post 8 (Kabir chat)
- Post 2 (2am fight) → Post 9 (Mira chat)

### 3. Coach Introduction Posts
AI-generated cinematic photo of the coach. Left 1/3rd = text. Right 2/3rd = coach photo facing slightly left. Open negative space on left for copy.

**Mira:** "Fights jo khatam nahi hote. / Ghar waale jo samajhte nahi."

## Viral Hook Format (For Reels)

The single best-performing format:
```
"5 [things / signs / reasons] that [outcome]"
```

**Rule:** Negativity and fear convert better than positivity.
- ✅ "5 signs your relationship is slowly draining you"
- ❌ "5 signs you are doing great in your relationship"

**Exception:** Reassurance content also goes viral ("5 signs it is okay to not have it all figured out").

### 7-Second Video Structure
- 0–2s: Motion hook (something moves in the first 1.8 seconds — never static)
- 2–5s: Hook text on screen the entire time
- 5s: Payoff line — "You don't have to figure this out alone."
- 5–7s: "read description below..." (drives replays)

### 6 Viral Patterns (Check all before posting)
1. Something moves in first 1.8 seconds
2. Cut rhythm ~2.3 seconds consistent
3. ONE emotional register throughout (validation / curiosity / fear / reassurance / tension)
4. ONE dominant subject per frame
5. Audio syncs to visual transition
6. Payoff lands at ~70% of clip duration (not at the very end)

## AI Image Generation

**Tool:** Nanobanana (with reference photo for face matching)

**Prompt structure for coach photos:**
> Full body shot of the [man/woman] in the reference photo, [pose] in [environment]. Positioned in the right 1/3rd of the frame, body angled slightly toward the left, looking [expression] at camera. Left 2/3rds is open negative space. Wearing [clothing]. [Lighting]. Shot on 85mm lens, shallow depth of field, cinematic, hyperrealistic photography. Keep the face identical to the reference. Do not alter facial features.

**Coach environments:**
- Mira → Warm coaching office, bookshelves, natural light from window
- Kabir → Warm coffee shop, Edison bulb lighting, wooden interiors
- Maya → Cozy apartment, sitting on couch, warm soft lighting, indoor plants

**For Reels/video:** Use Higgsfield (Seedance 2.0) to generate Indian-specific cinematic clips with native audio sync.

## Meta Ads Strategy

**Platform:** Meta (Facebook + Instagram)
**Budget (exploration phase):** ₹600–800/day across all ads
**Per ad:** ₹50–150/day during testing, 7–10 days to find winners

**Campaign structure:**
- 1 Campaign (App Installs)
- 5 Ad Sets: Mira / Kabir / Maya / Anya / General
- 2 ads per ad set (English + Hinglish versions)

**Key metrics:**
- CTR: >1.5% good
- CPC: ₹5–20 normal in India
- CPI target: below ₹40–60
- Kill ads above ₹80 CPI after 7 days
- Don't judge before Day 5 (Meta learning phase = 3–4 days)

**Business model:** ₹99/month subscription. CPPU must be below ₹1,000. Need 12+ month retention for profitability.
